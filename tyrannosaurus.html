Tyrannosaurus rex

Name meaning: Tyrant lizard
Heigth: 3.96m (13 ft)
Length: 12.3m (40 ft)
Weight: 8.4-14t
Diet: Herbivorous dinosaurs
Lived: Late Cretaceous (70 - 65 million years ago)
Fossil locations: North America

Description

Tyrannosaurus rex (also known as T. rex or T-Rex) was a bipedal carnivorous dinosaur living in the late Cretaceous era. Its most notable characteristics include a massive skull, large teeth, tiny forelimbs with two claws in each digit, large and powerful hind limbs and a long, heavy tail. Modern reconstructions have included feathers.

Old reconstructions of Tyrannosaurus rex have depicted it in an upright posture, with its tail sweeping the ground, similar to a kangaroo. However, later research has proved that Tyrannosaurus' posture was more balanced, with its head and tail held at around the same height.

Behavior

It is possible that Tyrannosaurus was a social animal, and hunted in packs. It may have been benefitial for Tyrannosauruses to hunt in packs, since prey such as Triceratops and Ankylosaurus had formidable defenses, making them harder to take down.

It's generally thought that Tyrannosaurus was both an active predator as well as a scavenger, like most large predators. Tyrannosaurus preyed upon armored herbivores such as ceratopsians and ankylosaurs, as well as hadrosaurs, and possibly sauropods. It has also been suspected that Tyrannosaurus was cannibalistic.

It's estimated that Tyrannosaurus had the second strongest bite among all terrestial animals, with a force of about 5 440kg (12 000 lb psi), second only to Deinosuchus. It is with this massive bite force that Tyrannosaurus would kill its prey, effortlessly shattering bone.

Tyrannosaurus' senses of smell, sight and hearing were all excellent.
